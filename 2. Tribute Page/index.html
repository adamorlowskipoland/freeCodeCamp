Howard Thurman

Great civil rights leader

Key mentor to leaders, including Martin Luther King, Jr.

Howard Thurman with his book "the growing edge", printed in 1956.

Here's a time line of Howard Thurman:

  • 1899 - born in Florida, probably in West Palm Beach.
  • 1923 - Thurman graduated from Morehouse College as valedictorian.
  • 1925 - he was ordained as a Baptist minister at First Baptist Church of Roanoke, Virginia.
  • 1928 - he moved to Atlanta, Georgia.
  • 1929 - Thurman pursued further study as a special student at Haverford College with Rufus Jones, a noted Quaker philosopher and mystic.
  • 1932-1944 - Thurman served as dean of Rankin Chapel at Howard University.
  • 1944 - he co-founded, along with Alfred Fisk, the first major interracial, interdenominational church in the United States.
  • 1949 - wrote "Jesus and the Disinherited", which deeply influenced Martin Luther King, Jr. and other leaders, both black and white, of the modern Civil Rights Movement
  • 1953-1965 - served as dean of Marsh Chapel at Boston University
  • 1981 - died on April 10, 1981 in San Francisco, California.
